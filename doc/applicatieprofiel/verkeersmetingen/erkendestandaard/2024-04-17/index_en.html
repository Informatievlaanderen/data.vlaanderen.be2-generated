This document describes anapplication profile, in this case OSLO Traffic measurement (application profile). The application profile specifies a semantic data model covering a set of usecases. The specification consists of terms with their constraints (cardinalities, codelists, usage guidelines).

Summary

This application profile aims to standardize data related to traffic measurements in order to exchange this data more smoothly and increase its reusability.

The data model builds on the AP Observations and Measurements (based on ISO 19156) and the AP Sensors and Sampling (based on SSN/SOSA). There are further references to INSPIRE Transportation Network and ISO 19157 Data quality.

Central to this is the class Traffic Measurement that indicates which Traffic Characteristic of which Traffic Object was measured and what the result was.

By traffic we mean the movement of objects such as people or goods over media such as road, waterway, railroad etc. in this profile the focus is on road traffic.

Objects related to traffic are the objects that move or are moved such as Vehicles and Traffic participants such as drivers, passengers, pedestrians. Of certainly equal importance are the objects over which traffic occurs.

To observe Traffic Characteristics of the objects being moved, classes such as Vehicle and Traffic Participant are necessary. Examples of characteristics in that case are speed, license plate and the like.

In order to describe characteristics related to traffic over the objects over which the traffic occurs, Road Objects such as Road Segments and Road Objects are needed, as well as Direction of Travel and Road Parts such as Lanes, Bicycle Paths and Footpaths. Examples of characteristics are traffic intensity, turn movement and the like, broken down by Vehicle or Traffic Participant type if necessary.

All these objects together are called Traffic Domain Objects.

Direct observation of objects involved in traffic is not always possible, e.g. one cannot follow an individual vehicle or observe a road as a whole. One has to sample these objects as it were, e.g. by positioning oneself at a certain point along the road (Traffic Monitoring Point) or monitoring a representative distance (Traffic Monitoring Trail). We call these objects Traffic Sampling Objects.

The geographical position of the measurement point or section can be specified as well as the Road Object or Road Section along or on which it is located (necessary if the sampled object is a Vehicle or Traffic Participant to say over which Road Object or Road Section it moves).

Traffic measurements are often exchanged in groups, e.g. all measurements of the same Measuring Point. The class Traffic measurement set allows grouping, e.g. to describe a time series.

BicyclePath

Description
Section of roadway intended exclusively for bicycle traffic.
Usage
Is not part of the carriageway (the carriageway is the part of the roadarea designed for vehicle traffic), e.g. cars are not allowed to drive or station on a BicyclePath. ATTENTION: Not to be confused with bicycle lanes (part of the carriageway) or with the side lanes of the central carriageway (not part of the carriageway but on which cars may drive to avoid oncoming traffic). BicyclePaths can be adjacent to the carriageway or separate or even have their own road Lane (in the latter case they are represented by their own RoadSegment).

Lane

Description
The carriageway is the part of the roadarea designed for a Vehicle to drive over. By dividing the carriageway you get a more controlled traffic flow, firstly because the driving directions or, for example, through and turning traffic are separated and also by limiting the width so that only one Vehicle can pass through one time. Lanes are typically designated by road markings, for example a white continuous or interrupted line. NOTE: Sidewalks or BicyclePaths are not Lanes as they are not intended for a car to drive on and are therefore not part of the carriageway.

TrafficMeasurement

Description
Determining the value of a certain feature of the traffic at a certain point in time or between two points in time.
Usage
Traffic is defined as the movement of objects such as people, freight, data, etc. over modes such as land, water, air, cables. In the context of this application profile, by traffic we mean road traffic. Traffic can refer to objects that move themselves (with or without a means of transport) or objects that are transported, but also to objects over which traffic takes place. The measurement can be the determination of the value of a feature of an individual Vehicle or TrafficParticipant during its movement (e.g. its speed or license plate) or of a feature of the infrastructure such as a RoadSegment or a Lane over which the traffic moves (e.g. the traffic intensity ). TrafficMeasurements are Observations, in other words there is a certain margin of error on the value due to the measuring technique used.

TrafficSamplingObject

Description
Object that is representative of the object for which the TrafficFeature is measured.
Usage
To be used if one cannot observe this object directly. For example, to determine the speed of a Vehicle, it is impossible to track each vehicle. Instead, one sets up a measuring point along the road to see how fast passing Vehicles are driving. Similar to, for example, traffic intensity: the passages of Vehicles are counted at a measuring point along the road and it is assumed that this figure is representative of at least the part of the road between two consecutive intersections (being the corresponding RoadSegment). ATTENTION: This class is abstract, it may not be instantiated. Use the subclasses TrafficMeasurementPoint or TrafficMeasurementSection instead.
