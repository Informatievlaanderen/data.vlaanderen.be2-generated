This document describes a vocabulary, in this case BO Observations (Vocabulary). A vocabulary is a collection of reusable terms, namely classes and properties, whose meaning is recorded through a label and definition. For terms defined in this domain, additional formal agreements are available. For external terms, the formal agreements are copied from the external source.

Summary

The OSLO Soil & Subsurface Observations vocabulary includes terms to describe more specialized Soil and Subsurface Observations.

These are Observations that cannot be described generically with the terms from the vocabulary OSLO Sensors and Sampling.

The observations in question are analyses that usually take place in a lab with specialized equipment to determine the Grain Distribution or geotechnical characteristics of Soil Samples such as Compressibility, Permeability or Shear Resistance.

These are often specializations of measurement series, i.e. the result varies according to another parameter such as load, pressure or grain size.

A term is also provided for Borehole measurements.

Terminology for the soil samples on which the analyses are based is to be found in the vocabulary OSLO Soil Drilling.
