This document describes a vocabulary, in this case OSLO Cycling Infrastructure (Vocabulary). A vocabulary is a collection of reusable terms, namely classes and properties, whose meaning is recorded through a label and definition. For terms defined in this domain, additional formal agreements are available. For external terms, the formal agreements are copied from the external source.

Summary

The vocabulary "OSLO-Cycling Infrastructure" defines the terms that apply to information sharing about the infrastructure relevant to the cyclist, the network that the cyclist uses to get around and the properties that describe the network.

Infrastructure refers to objects that are located in the public domain, such as bicycle counting poles, traffic signs and bicycle parking.

The network presents the logical side that presents all bicycle facilities (cycle paths, cycle paths, cycle streets) as a concatenation of knots, segments, sequences and sets.

The characteristics are concepts that describe the bicycle network, such as the width, the layout and the direction of traffic.

2. Conformity

An exchange of data, by whatever means, is subject to this vocabulary when it uses the terminology (classes and properties) in a manner consistent with the semantics established in the latest version of the specification (domain, scope, definition and usage) and does not use terminology from other vocabularies as an alternative to the proposed terminology included in this vocabulary.
