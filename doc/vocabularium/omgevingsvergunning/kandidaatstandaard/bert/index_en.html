Dependencies

This document describes a vocabulary, in this case Environmental permit. A vocabulary is a collection of reusable terms, namely classes and properties, whose meaning is recorded through a label and definition. For terms defined in this domain, additional formal agreements are available. For external terms, the formal agreements are copied from the external source.

Summary

The Environmental Permit Vocabulary provides a standardised core conceptual framework for the environmental permit domain. The vocabulary should promote the exchange and interconnection (interoperability) of authorisation information, and contribute to a clear common understanding and the avoidance of misunderstandings and miscommunication between stakeholders, including a variety of public authorities and the wider public.

The vocabulary is certainly not all-encompassing, in the sense that it does not encapsulate the entire domain in detail.

The vocabulary focuses mainly on the legal core of the matter: the "backbone" of environmental permit policy, so to speak. In short, these are the legal acts and legal facts that produce and evolve the licensed situation, their legal-substantive significance with regard to acts and activities that are regulated under the environmental permit policy, and the various qualitative and spatial-temporal dimensions that form the main starting points for questioning and linking.

The vocabulary leaves the necessary room for ad-hoc expansion, combination with other vocabularies, and possible further, more focused standardization in the future.
