This document describes a vocabulary, in this case Soil and subsoil (vocabulary). A vocabulary is a collection of reusable terms, namely classes and properties, whose meaning is recorded through a label and definition. For terms defined in this domain, additional formal agreements are available. For external terms, the formal agreements are copied from the external source.

Summary

The Soil and Subsoil vocabulary adds terminology to the Observations and Measurements and Sensors and Sampling vocabulary specifically for the Soil and Subsoil domain.

The terms are generic enough to be useful for multiple subdomains within Soil & Subsoil.

It concerns terms such as Depth, Ground level or an extension of Profile from Observations and Measurements with Profile Element and Low as a specialization.

Measurement series and fraction measurement as a specialization have also been added as a supplement to Discrete Ranges from Observations and Measurements. The latter are limited to variations of a phenomenon in time and space, while in this domain it must also be possible to describe variations in function of other parameters.

also some utilitarian classes have been added such as assignment and Acknowledgment.

In addition to Ranges that can document variations of phenomena in space and time, there was also a need for "ordinary" measurement series in the domain of Soil and Subsurface, where the phenomenon can be a function of an arbitrary parameter. For that reason, we added Measurement Series and the widely used Fraction Measurements specialization.

An exchange of data, by whatever means, is subject to this vocabulary when it uses the terminology (classes and properties) in a manner consistent with the semantics established in the latest version of the specification (domain, scope, definition and usage) and does not use terminology from other vocabularies as an alternative to the proposed terminology included in this vocabulary.
